# Metronome

The built-in metronome gives you a steady click track to practice to, without needing a separate app.

---

## Opening the metronome

Click the **Metronome** tab in the navigation bar.

---

## Setting the tempo

- Type a BPM value directly into the **BPM field**.
- Click the **+ / −** buttons to nudge the tempo up or down by 1 BPM.
- Use **Tap Tempo** — click the **Tap** button repeatedly in time with the beat you want. After three or four taps, the BPM field updates to match your average tap interval.

---

## Starting and stopping

Click **Play** to start the click. Click **Stop** to stop it. The metronome continues running in the background while you switch to other tabs.

---

## Adjusting the click sound

| Control | What it does |
|---------|--------------|
| **Volume** | How loud the click is in your mix (0–100%) |
| **Pan** | Positions the click left or right in the stereo field — useful if you want the click on one side only |

---

## Host sync (DAW plugin mode)

When Soundshed Guitar is running as a plugin inside a DAW, the metronome can sync to the DAW's transport:

1. Enable **Host Sync** in the metronome panel.
2. The BPM and play/stop controls are now driven by your DAW — the metronome starts when you press Play in the DAW and matches the project tempo.

When host sync is active, the manual BPM field is read-only and shows the tempo coming from the DAW.

---

## Tips

- Start slow — set the BPM 10–20% slower than your target and build up speed gradually. The metronome makes it obvious when you rush or drag.
- Use the pan control to keep the click out of a recording. Pan it hard to one side and record only the opposite channel if your setup allows it.
